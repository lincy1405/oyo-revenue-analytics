# Insights & Recommendations Report
## OYO Hotels — Revenue & Occupancy Analytics

**Prepared By:** Lincy DCosta  
**Date:** May 2026

---

## Executive Summary
Analysis of 325 OYO properties across 15 Indian cities revealed that average occupancy stands at 70.18% — below OYO's 75% target. Total annual revenue across all properties is ₹245M. This report identifies 5 key insights with actionable recommendations to improve occupancy, RevPAR, and reduce cancellations.

---

## Insight 1: Mumbai and Delhi Drive 35%+ of Total Revenue
**Finding:** Mumbai leads with ₹40M+ in revenue followed closely by Delhi. Tier 1 cities (Mumbai, Delhi, Bangalore, Chennai, Hyderabad) collectively contribute over 60% of total revenue despite representing only 33% of cities.

**Recommendation:** Prioritize Tier 1 city expansion — add 10-15 new properties in Mumbai and Delhi in high-demand corporate corridors. Implement dynamic pricing during peak business travel seasons (October-March) to maximize RevPAR in these markets.

---

## Insight 2: Only 36.78% of Properties Achieve High Occupancy
**Finding:** 25.03% of properties are in the Low occupancy category (below 50%) — meaning 1 in 4 properties is severely underperforming. Only 36.78% meet the High occupancy threshold of 75%+.

**Recommendation:** Conduct a property-level audit of all Low occupancy properties. Key interventions: reduce base pricing by 15-20%, improve listing quality on OTA platforms, and consider rebranding low-performing OYO Rooms properties to Capital O for better positioning.

---

## Insight 3: Goa Delivers Highest RevPAR Despite Fewer Properties
**Finding:** Goa consistently delivers the highest RevPAR among all cities, driven by premium seasonal pricing during October-March. Despite having fewer properties than metro cities, Goa's revenue per room far exceeds the national average.

**Recommendation:** Expand Goa's premium property portfolio — add OYO Townhouse and Palette properties to capture higher-paying leisure travelers. Implement aggressive dynamic pricing during peak season (December-February) where rates can be 40-50% above base price.

---

## Insight 4: Occupancy Drops Sharply in June-August
**Finding:** Seasonal analysis shows occupancy dipping significantly in summer months (May-August) across most cities except Goa (which peaks in winter). This creates a revenue gap of approximately 25-30% compared to peak months.

**Recommendation:** Launch targeted summer promotions — corporate staycation packages, extended stay discounts, and family travel deals during June-August. Partner with companies for employee travel programs to fill rooms during off-peak periods.

---

## Insight 5: 15.10% Average Cancellation Rate Is Too High
**Finding:** The average cancellation rate of 15.10% means OYO loses approximately 1 in 7 confirmed bookings. This directly impacts revenue predictability and room utilization.

**Recommendation:** Implement a tiered cancellation policy — free cancellation up to 48 hours, 50% charge within 24 hours. Introduce non-refundable discounted rates (10-15% cheaper) to incentivize committed bookings. Use predictive analytics to overbooking by 5-8% in high-cancellation periods.
